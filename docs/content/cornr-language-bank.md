## Share Moment Rules

The share quote is Cornr's only viral mechanic in v1. The essence line must be:
- **Flattering, not describing.** "You notice things others walk past" ✓ / "You prefer minimalist design" ✗
- **Specific enough to feel personal, vague enough to apply broadly.** The Barnum sweet spot.
- **Speakable.** If a user imagines saying "I'm The Curator — [essence line]" to a friend, it must feel natural, not pretentious.
- **NOT a horoscope.** "Your intuition guides your choices" ✗ / "You'd rather have three perfect things than thirty okay ones" ✓
- **8-14 words.** Locked in Phase 1 brief. Check length before falling in love with a line.

## In-App vs External Voice

The language bank primarily feeds **in-app copy** — casual, FTB-native, "getting sorted" register.

**External copy** (App Store, social, landing page) uses a slightly more elevated register:
- In-app: "Let's get you sorted" → External: "Make your first home yours"
- In-app: "Getting settled" → External: "Your style, your home, your way"
- Both are warm, both are Cornr, but external assumes no existing relationship.

The tagline "Every corner, considered" bridges both registers — it works on the App Store and on the loading screen.

## Jargon to Plain English

Interior-designer terms FTBs don't use. Avoid these in all user-facing copy:
- "Anchors the space" → "ties the room together" or "makes it feel finished"
- "Statement piece" → "the one that gets noticed" or "the one people ask about"
- "Palette" → "colours"
- "Scheme" → "look" or "feel"
- "Complementary" → "goes with" or "works with"
- "Transitional space" → "hallway" or "landing"
- "Negative space" → don't reference
- "Juxtaposition" → never
- "Collected" → "chosen" (too close to "curated")

Note: "accent" (as in accent wall, accent colour) is widely understood and fine to use.

## Important Notes

- **Budget tier names** ("High Street Finds" / "Great Quality" / "Invest Well") are **provisional until proven** with first 100 post-launch users. Do not treat as settled.
- **Per-archetype suggestions below are inspiration, not draft copy.** Every description must go through the Phase 1 brief process (4-component structure, Barnum-effect defence, 60-110 words) before shipping.
- **Trades cluster language is research evidence, not copy direction.** The extreme emotional language (panic attacks, medication) validates the pain but must never appear in user-facing copy, even indirectly. The trades tab acknowledges pain through its solution ("Trusted trades who get your style"), not by naming the pain.

## Cluster 1: The Overwhelm
**Where people are when they find Cornr. Maps to: onboarding, error states, empty states.**

Real quotes and paraphrases:
- "I don't know where to start"
- "There's so many decisions"
- "I can't afford to get it wrong"
- "We've been in three months and still haven't got a sofa"
- "Does this go?" (never "does this match my aesthetic")
- "I know what I like when I see it but I can't explain it"
- "Pinterest is just making it worse — more ideas but no answers"
- "Every room needs everything and I can only do one thing at a time"
- "I feel like I should know this stuff but I don't"
- "My partner and I can't agree on anything"

**Cornr copy principle:** Never add to the overwhelm. Every screen should reduce the number of decisions, not increase them. Copy should feel like a friend who's already done this, not an expert lecturing.

**Specific applications:**
- Onboarding subtext: "so we can get your [Archetype] picks right" (Cornr takes responsibility)
- Error states: "We're finding your picks — give us a moment" (calm, not technical)
- Empty states: "Your [room_type] is waiting" (forward-looking, not accusatory)
- Escape hatch: "Not sure this is your style? Find out in 2 minutes" (answers the doubt)

## 2. The Progress Cluster
**The language of moving forward. Maps to: room setup, sequencing, room progress.**

Real quotes and paraphrases:
- "Getting sorted" / "getting the house sorted"
- "Getting settled" / "are you settled in yet?"
- "Making it ours" / "making it feel like home"
- "One room at a time"
- "We did the living room first, then the bedroom"
- "It's coming together"
- "We're getting there"
- "Just got my keys" (the emotional peak of completion)
- "House to home" (the transformation arc)

**Cornr copy principle:** Frame every interaction as progress, not work. Users should feel like they're moving forward, not filling in forms.

**Specific applications:**
- Journey stage options: "Just got my keys", "A few months in, getting sorted", "Settled in"
- Room setup bridge: "Let's get your first [Archetype] picks" (action, not administration)
- Room progress: "Your next step: Lighting" (forward momentum)
- Post-recommendation: "Before we fine-tune — anything you're keeping?" (refinement, not interrogation)
- Completion moments: "Your [room_type] is coming together" (echoes their own language)

## 3. The Budget Anxiety Cluster
**Fear of wasting money. Maps to: recommendation rationale, budget tier copy, product cards.**

Real quotes and paraphrases:
- "I can't afford to buy something and then hate it"
- "We spent £35K and I still don't know if it was right"
- "Is it worth spending more on the sofa?"
- "We went cheap on the first sofa and regretted it within a year"
- "IKEA for now, upgrade later"
- "How much should I actually spend on a bed?"
- "The delivery was 8 weeks and nobody told us"
- "I bought three sets of curtains before I got the right ones"

**Cornr copy principle:** Every recommendation should address the money fear, not just the taste match. "This is right for you" is half the answer. "This is right for you AND worth the spend" is the whole answer.

**Specific applications:**
- AI rationale: "Chosen because it fits your Curator eye for clean surfaces — and it's built to last" (taste + durability)
- Delivery tier: "Typically arrives in days" / "Made to order — worth the wait" (honesty + reassurance)
- Budget tier descriptions: Don't use "budget" / "mid" / "premium". Use the existing tier names that tested well: "High Street Finds" / "Great Quality" / "Invest Well"
- Sequencing: "This is where your budget works hardest in this room" (validates the spend)
- Post-purchase (v2): "Good choice — 78% of Curators who bought this kept it" (social proof on confidence)

## 4. The Trades Terror Cluster
**Extreme emotional language. Maps to: trades tab, v2 trades positioning, trust signals.**

Real quotes and paraphrases:
- "Finding a good tradesperson is a nightmare"
- "Word of mouth is the only way I'd trust anyone"
- "I was having panic attacks about the builder"
- "We had three different plumbers and none of them showed up"
- "Checkatrade is pay-to-play — you can't trust the reviews"
- "My marriage nearly ended over the kitchen renovation"
- "I ended up on medication because of the stress"
- "Never again. I'd rather live with it broken"

**Cornr copy principle:** The trades tab "Coming Soon" copy must acknowledge this pain without trivialising it. When trades launches in v2, the positioning must lead with trust, not convenience.

**Specific applications:**
- Trades tab: Don't say "Find tradespeople." Say "Trusted trades who get your style." The word "trusted" matters more than anything else.
- Waitlist email: "We're building something better. We'll let you know when it's ready." (Not "Coming soon!" which sounds like every other app that never delivers.)
- v2 trades positioning: "Matched to your taste, vetted by Cornr" (archetype connection + trust signal)

## 5. The Identity Cluster
**How people describe the home they want. Maps to: archetype descriptions, sensory anchors, share quotes.**

Real quotes and paraphrases:
- "I know what I like when I see it" (THE universal quote)
- "I want it to feel warm but not cluttered"
- "Scandi but with more personality"
- "Not a show home"
- "Like it's been collected over time, not bought all at once"
- "I want people to walk in and feel comfortable"
- "Cosy but grown-up"
- "Natural but not rustic"
- "Modern but not cold"
- "I don't want it to look like everyone else's"

**Cornr copy principle:** The archetype reveal is the moment where "I know what I like but can't explain it" becomes "oh, that's exactly it." The description must give them words they'll use outside Cornr.

**Specific applications per archetype (inspiration — not draft copy, must go through Phase 1 brief):**
- Curator: mirrors "chosen over time" → "Each piece looks like you found it, not just bought it"
- Traditionalist: mirrors "cosy but grown-up" → "Warm without being twee, heritage without being museum"
- Free Spirit: mirrors "not like everyone else's" → "Your home looks like no one else's — and that's the point"
- Purist: mirrors "modern but not cold" → "Clean lines, but never sterile. Every surface breathes"
- Maker: mirrors "natural but not rustic" → "Raw materials, honest textures, nothing pretending to be something it's not"
- Nester: mirrors "walk in and feel comfortable" → "People relax the moment they step in. That's not an accident"
- Modernist: mirrors "Scandi but with more personality" → "Restrained enough to feel calm, bold enough to feel yours"

## 6. The Renter Cluster
**Constraint-driven creativity. Maps to: renter journey_stage, renter-friendly recommendations.**

Real quotes and paraphrases:
- "I can't drill into the walls"
- "Command hooks are my best friend"
- "I want it to feel like mine but I know we'll have to move"
- "Peel-and-stick everything"
- "Freestanding is the only option"
- "The landlord won't let us change anything"
- "Rugs, lamps, cushions — that's all we've got to work with"
- "Making the best of what we've got"
- "Take it with you when you go"

**Cornr copy principle:** Renter constraints are creative constraints, not limitations. The copy should feel empowering, not sympathetic.

**Specific applications:**
- Renter recommendations: "Works beautifully without modification" (positive framing)
- Renter room setup: No different UI, just different defaults — prompt prioritises portable, freestanding items
- Renter sequencing: "Rug first (ties the room together without touching the floor), then lamp (sets the mood without touching the ceiling), then art (leans, doesn't hang)"
- NOT: "Because you're renting, you can't..." — NEVER frame as limitation
- Instead: "Portable pieces that come with you to your next place" (forward-looking, empowering)

## Usage Rules

1. This language bank feeds Sprint 2 T4 archetype descriptions directly. Read the identity cluster before writing any description.
2. The overwhelm and progress clusters feed all onboarding copy. Read before writing journey_stage labels and room setup bridging text.
3. The budget anxiety cluster feeds Sprint 3 recommendation rationale prompt instructions. Read before designing the Haiku prompt.
4. The renter cluster feeds the renting journey_stage prompt branch. Read before writing renter-specific prompt instructions.
5. Never use verbatim quotes as Cornr copy — the language bank is a register guide, not a copy-paste resource. Match the emotional register, not the exact words.
6. The BDS v3 "Never Say" list still applies. Don't let the language bank override voice rules — "curated" is still banned even though users don't say it either.
